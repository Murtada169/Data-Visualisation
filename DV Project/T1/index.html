Average Students' Subject Score and Their Parents' Education

The grouped bar chart drawn above shows the average student score in a particular subject according to their parents' education.

  • Students whose parents have had a formal education have the highest average score while students whose parents have had a non-formal education have the lowest average score.
  • The subject with the highest average score is ASScience2 for students whose parents had a formal education with an average of 36.
  • The subject with the lowest average score is ASMath1 and ASMath2 for students whose parents had a non-formal education with an average of 24.

Mother Education Effect on Student's Performance

0-10 -> "Dark Blue", 10-20 ->"Orange", 20-30 ->"Grey", 30-40 ->"Yellow" , 40-50 ->"Sky Blue" , 50-60 ->"Green")

The stacked bar chart above shows the relation between mothers level of education towards the child's academic performance. For reference the scores have been given ranges/subgroups from 0-60 and that is plotted using different colors in the stacked bars. The x axis is the level of mothers education from 1-4 ( primary to higher education) Y axis refers to the sum of scores. As we can see this graph clearly indicates that with rising level of mothers education the children performce generally increases whose mothers are well educated.

A point to realise is that, as the education level increases the higher GPA interval sizes increase and vice versa

Absence Effect on Performance

This graph refers to the relation between student absences in class with student performance.
The X axis clearly indicates the amount of absences by a student which is divided into subgroups/ranges from 0-60. Y axis on the first graph refers to the max score for each group of absences and the Y axis on the second graph refers to the avg score of each group of absences.
Both of the graphs indicate that a higher absences rate leads to less scores in academics. The group with the max score is the absence group of 0-10 absences and the scores go down as the absences are increased which can be seen by looking at the graph's trend and the max score of the absence group of 50-60 which is almost 35 and the highest scores of less absences can go upto even above 55.

Family Status Effect on Student Performance

0-10 -> "Fern Green", 10-20 ->"Red", 20-30 ->"Orange", 30-40 ->"Parrot Green" , 40-50 ->"Brown" , 50-60 ->"Purple")

In todays world we have heard that a happy family plays a vital role in the success of a child.
Using this grouped bar chart we can see that. A set of data was collected from a school in Portugal which shows the impact of divorced parents on the Childs academic performance. The parents apart have been mentioned as (A) on the X axis and together has been mentioned with a (T). On the Y axis are the number students which fall into different groups of scores i.e the subgroups are ranged from 0-60.
The subgroups are referred to as the color of each bar. We can clearly see that the parents who are together their children perform better in academics and more number students in the (T) part lie inside the high ranges scores.
